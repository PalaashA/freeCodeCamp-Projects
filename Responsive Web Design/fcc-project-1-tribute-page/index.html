Sir Isaac Newton

The "Natural Philosopher"
Portrait of Sir Isaac Newton by Barrington Bramley

Sir Isaac Newton (25 December 1642 – 20 March 1726/27) was an English mathematician, physicist, astronomer, theologian, and author (described in his own day as a "natural philosopher") who is widely recognised as one of the most influential scientists of all time, and a key figure in the scientific revolution. His book Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica ("Mathematical Principles of Natural Philosophy"), first published in 1687, laid the foundations of classical mechanics. Newton also made seminal contributions to optics, and shares credit with Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz for developing the infinitesimal calculus.

A timeline of Newton's Life :

  • 1642: Born in Lincolnshire, England
  • 1661: Admitted to Trinity College, Cambridge
  • 1665: Discovered generalised Binomial Theorem, which later helped theorize Calculus
  • 1667: Returned to Cambridge after temporary leave. Also elected as fellow for Trinity
  • 1669: Appointed "Lucasian Professor of Mathematics"
  • 1670-1672: Lectured in Optics. Investigated Refraction of Light, demonstrating that the multicoloured spectrum produced by a prism could be recomposed into white light by a lens and a second prism
  • 1668: Produced the first Reflecting Telescope
  • 1675: Newton posited the existence of the ether to transmit forces between particles in "Hypothesis of Light"
  • 1687: Published "Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica"
  • 1703: President of Royal Society and an associate of the French Académie des Sciences
  • 1704: Newton published "Opticks"
  • 1727: Died at the age of 85
